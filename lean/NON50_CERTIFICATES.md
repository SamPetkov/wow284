# Lean spectral certificates for the non-50 WOW-284 constructions

This note records the exact proof surface for the explicit counterexamples of
orders 38, 39, 40, and 42. It distinguishes the finite spectral claims proved
in Lean from the graph-theoretic and stronger spectral facts proved elsewhere
in the repository.

## Common proof architecture

Every construction is an explicitly labelled induced subgraph of the
Hoffman–Singleton coordinate graph, or of the 40-vertex induced subgraph. The
Lean developments reuse the inherited adjacency relation and contain separate
finite lemmas for symmetry, irreflexivity, triangle exclusion, 4-cycle
exclusion, a displayed length-five closed walk, and paths of length at most
three.

For orders 38, 39, and 42, Lean defines a finite semantic matrix `D` by the
explicit `0/1/2/3` adjacency formula and proves the bounded-path facts used by
that formula. The generated integer matrix is reconstructed independently by
breadth-first search in Python, and Lean proves entrywise equality with the
semantic definition.

No floating-point eigenvalue is used in any formal endpoint. Large rational
matrix identities are generated exactly, cleared into bounded finite goals, and
split into small modules so that kernel checking remains within hosted-CI
resources.

## Order 38

Construction: delete the adjacent vertices `P_(1,0)` and `P_(1,1)` from the
40-vertex graph.

Formal endpoint:

- degree profile `5^10 6^28`;
- exact minimum dual degree `17/3`, proved by a universal lower bound and an
  explicit attaining vertex;
- exact rational LDL certificate for `3D + 17I`;
- positive definiteness of `3D + 17I` over both `Q` and `R`;
- hence every real eigenpair `(mu,x)` of the formal matrix `Dr` with `x ≠ 0`
  satisfies `mu > -17/3`;
- therefore `17/3 + mu > 0` for every such eigenpair.

The stronger exact equality `lambda_min(D) = -3 - sqrt(7)` is proved by exact
Sturm computation in the analytic artifact, but is not needed for the Lean
counterexample endpoint.

Public theorem: `Wow284.Induced38.counterexample_endpoint`.

## Order 39

Construction: delete `P_(1,0)` from the 40-vertex graph.

Formal endpoint generated by `scripts/generate_lean39_42.py`:

- degree profile `5^6 6^33`;
- exact minimum dual degree `35/6`;
- exact rational LDL certificate for `6D + 35I`;
- positive definiteness of `6D + 35I` over both `Q` and `R`;
- hence every real eigenpair `(mu,x)` of the formal matrix `Dr` with `x ≠ 0`
  satisfies `mu > -35/6`;
- therefore `35/6 + mu > 0` for every such eigenpair.

Public theorem: `Wow284.Induced39.counterexample_endpoint`.

## Order 40

Construction: delete the induced Petersen graph
`{P_(0,j), Q_(0,j) : j in F_5}` from the Hoffman–Singleton graph.

Formal endpoint:

- 6-regularity and therefore dual degree six at every vertex;
- semantic distance matrix equal to the exact generated matrix;
- an invertible rational eigenbasis with both inverse identities;
- exact diagonalization with diagonal entries `-5`, `0`, `3`, and `75`;
- multiplicities `18`, `16`, `5`, and `1` respectively;
- proof that `-5` is the attained minimum entry of the diagonal form;
- exact strict gap `6 + (-5) = 1`.

Public theorem: `Wow284.Induced40.exact_counterexample_certificate`.

## Order 42

Construction: take the second subconstituent of `P_(0,0)` in the
Hoffman–Singleton graph; equivalently, delete that vertex and its seven
neighbours.

Formal endpoint generated by `scripts/generate_lean39_42.py`:

- 6-regularity and exact minimum dual degree six;
- exact rational LDL certificate for `D + 6I`;
- positive definiteness of `D + 6I` over both `Q` and `R`;
- hence every real eigenpair `(mu,x)` of the formal matrix `Dr` with `x ≠ 0`
  satisfies `mu > -6`;
- therefore `6 + mu > 0` for every such eigenpair.

The analytic artifact additionally gives the exact distance spectrum
`{81^1, 4^6, 0^14, (-5)^21}`. The positive-definiteness endpoint is sufficient
for the formal refutation and avoids duplicating a full 42-column eigenbasis.

Public theorem: `Wow284.Induced42.counterexample_endpoint`.

## Generation and checking

The order-38 and order-40 generated files are committed and checked for
freshness. The order-39 and order-42 files are generated deterministically in
CI to keep the repository diff smaller:

```text
python scripts/generate_lean39_42.py
cd lean
lake build Wow284Extension
lake env lean Wow284Generated3942.lean
lake env lean Wow284ExtensionAudit.lean
lake env lean Wow284Generated3942Audit.lean
```

The generator can run its exact graph, dual-degree, distance, and LDL
calculations without writing files:

```text
python scripts/generate_lean39_42.py --verify-only
```

The static audit rejects imported occurrences of `sorry`, `admit`,
`native_decide`, `bv_decide`, unsafe declarations, and new axioms. The two
axiom-report files expose the transitive trust surface of every public endpoint.

## Scope of the formal claim

For orders 38, 39, and 42, the public Lean theorem packages the exact minimum
dual-degree data, positive definiteness of the shifted formal matrix, and the
strict inequality for every nonzero real eigenpair of that matrix. It does not
invoke a generic least-eigenvalue API.

For order 40, the public Lean theorem packages a two-sided invertible
diagonalization, the exact diagonal-entry multiplicities, and the fact that
`-5` is the attained minimum diagonal entry. The current theorem does not
separately restate the corresponding generic least-eigenvalue conclusion.

The structural lemmas are not bundled into these public spectral endpoints,
and the current non-50 developments do not identify their semantic matrix with
Mathlib's `SimpleGraph.dist` in a single theorem. Accordingly, the precise
claim is **kernel-checked finite spectral certificate**, not a full
standard-library formalization of every graph-theoretic phrase in the
manuscript. The exact Python verifiers independently check the labelled graphs,
shortest-path distance matrices, dual degrees, and certificate identities.
