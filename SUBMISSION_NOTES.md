**Title:** Exact Counterexamples and Spectral Mechanisms for WOW-284

**Author:** Samuil Petkov

**Primary category:** `math.CO`

**Cross-list:** `math.SP`

**Comments:** 12 pages.

**License:** Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0)

**Abstract:**

> WOW-284 asserts that the minimum dual degree of every connected graph of
> order at least three and girth at least five is at most the negative of its
> least distance eigenvalue. We disprove the conjecture through exact
> counterexamples of orders 38, 39, 40, 42, and 50, all arising from
> Hoffman-Singleton coordinates. A common structural picture explains much of
> this phenomenon. For every connected k-regular graph of girth at least five
> and diameter three, we prove
> $D=3J+(k-3)I-2A-A^2$, so a nonprincipal adjacency eigenvalue $\theta$
> yields the distance eigenvalue $k-2-(\theta+1)^2$ and an exact criterion for
> strict failure. This mechanism organizes the regular diameter-three
> examples and a parameterized construction from Moore second
> subconstituents, while induced subgraphs produce further nonregular
> descendants. Exact enumeration proves that all 40 labelled single-vertex
> deletions and all 120 labelled edge-endpoint deletions of the 40-vertex
> graph are strict counterexamples. We also give exact positive and negative
> controls. The finite graph data and associated spectral certificates are
> checked by exact programs. The 50-vertex coordinate certificate and scalar
> Moore threshold are fully verified in Lean 4.31; Lean 4.31 also
> kernel-checks finite spectral certificates for the constructions of orders
> 38, 39, 40, and 42.
